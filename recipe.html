CHOCOFLAN (also refered to "The Impossible Cake")



*Source of picuter: comidamexicana.com/recetas/item/589/-choco-flan

Short Description:

Chocoflan is a Mexican flan which has three delicious layers. The top layer consists of caramel, the middle layer is of a sweet creamy flan, and the
bottom layer of a fluffy and moist chocolate cake. The two layers switch places while it's cooking, which is why it is nicknamed, "El Pastel Impossible"
(The Impossible Cake).

Below you will find a very easy recipe for this delicious, fun to make cake!

Ingredients:

For the carmel topping:

  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 3 tablespoons of water

For the flan:

  • 1 12oz can of evaporated milk
  • 1 14oz can of sweetened condensed milk
  • 1 8oz of cream cheese
  • 5 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup of sugar
  • 1/2 cup of heavy cream
  • 1 tablespoon of vanilla

For the cake:

  • 1 box of moist chocolate cake mix

Directions:

  1. Pre-heat oven to 375* F (180* C)

    2. Making The Carmel
  2. In a glass measuring cup (or micowave safe bowel), mix the sugar and water,
    and place in the microwave on high for 3 minutes. Take out and lightly stir syrup.
    (note: be very careful,as the glass will get hot during this process)
  3. Place back in microwave on high for 1 minute. We are wanting a carmel color
    (light-clear brownish color). Repeat this step until desired color is reached.
    (note: keep a close eye on the color as you do not want to over cook the carmel.)
  4. Once carmel color has been achieved, lightly spray cooking spray into cake pan, and
    immediately (but very carefully) pour hot carmel into the bottom of your sprayed pan.

    5. Making Flan
  5. Place all ingredients into a blender, and blend on medium speed until all ingredients
    are well blended.
  6. Pour all contents of the blender into your cake pan (over the carmel, which should be
    at the bottom of your pan already).

    7. Making The Chocolate Cake
  7. Follow the directions on the box.
  8. Pour (already prepared) cake mix into the cake pan which at this point should already
    contain your carmel, and flan mix. (notice how the cake mix goes to the bottom?)
  9. Bake for about 1-1/2 hours or until a knife inserted near the center comes out clean(ish).
    Cool completely in pan on wire rack, and then refrigerate for about 2 hours. Loosen from
    the sides of the pan, invert onto a plate, remove pan, and wallah. (What happened to the
    chocolate cake? Is it on top or on bottom? - pretty neat, huh).
  10. Serve and Enjoy! I know you will love it!

Hope you had fun making this cake. It's truly not as hard as the name makes it out to be, is it?
